# Empanada

# WORK IN PROGRESS 

## Main ingredient
400g frozen octopus but it can also be 300g of tuna or 350g of chicken

## Dough ingredients
- 500g all purpose flour
- Yeast
- 12 g of salt
- 150 ml of lukewarm water
- 150 ml of the oil from the sofrito

## Sofrito ingredients
- 1 large onion
- 1 Red pepper
- 1 Green pepper
- 3 cloves of garlic
- 2 ripe tomatoes
- 1 tablespoon of  paprika
- 150 ml of oil
- Salt

## Steps

Preheat the oven to 180º
Peel and chop the onion.
Peel the garlic cloves and chop them.
Cut the peppers into small pieces.
Grate the tomatoes.
Cut the octopus into small slices.
Elaboration
Preparation of the sofrito:
Pour the oil into a frying pan and place it over medium heat.

When the oil is hot, add the onion, garlic, and peppers, add a little salt, and sauté until the onion is translucent.

Then you add the sweet red paprika and sauté it for a few seconds before adding the grated tomato and give it a couple of turns.

Add the octopus and stir everything together, let it all sauté for a couple more minutes.

Pass the sofrito through a chinois strainer to collect the oil and leave the sofrito without excess oil.

Reserve about 150 ml of the oil from the sofrito, to use in making the dough.


Making the dough:
In a bowl, pour the wheat flour, add the salt, and with your hands, crumble the yeast so that it mixes well.

Now you stir it and gradually add the warm water and the oil from the sofrito, mixing it until you can knead it with your hands.

Now put some flour on the counter and pour in the dough, continue kneading vigorously for about 6 to 8 minutes.

You need to achieve an elastic and homogeneous dough like that of bread.

Make a ball with the dough and put it back in the bowl, cover it with a cloth and let it rest in a warm place for 1 hour.

After this time, take the dough and put it back on the counter, knead it with your fingertips to release the gases and let it rest for 5 minutes.

Now cut it in two, one part a little bigger for the base and another for the lid.

Take the base and roll it out with a rolling pin, trying to make it as thin as possible without breaking it.

Place the dough on the mold and adjust it to fit.

Cut off the excess dough with a knife, and with a fork make some holes in the base, so that it doesn't puff up.
Fill the base with the sofrito, spreading it well so that the entire base is covered with the filling.

Now do the same with the other piece of dough: roll it out with a rolling pin and cut it with a knife. You can use the mold as a template to cut the dough.

Cover the empanada with the dough and seal the lid well with the base so that it is well sealed.

You make a small hole in the center so it can breathe and decorate it to your liking.

Brush it with beaten egg and put it in the oven at 180º for approximately 40 or 45 minutes, until golden brown.

When you take it out of the oven, you can brush it with the oil from the sofrito to make it shine.

Let it cool down a little and then eat it.
