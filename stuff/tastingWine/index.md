## Tasting is about what we feel
Wine is a completely unnecessary beverage  and we drink it because of its intrinsic pleasure.  Many people try to transform it into a sport in which tasting certain note or identifying certain specific plots is desirable, but tasting wine is closer to enjoying piece of art than to performing well in a sports competition. If you smell pears in that red bottle or if you smell cat pee in that other one, it is your experience.

Wine is the most varied product you can eat or drink in terms of different chemical compounds. They interact with each other, they change with temperature, the smell, taste, and touch interact with each other, they interact with the food,... Each wine experience is unique to that person and that moment.

## Two or more

Tasting is about building a sense of position in the space. The more we taste, te easier it gets to locate ourselves in the space. Therefore, trying one drink every few days will reset the references and will make every wine taste like wine again.

A much faster way is to always open two or more wines and taste them on one sitting, which will allow a sense of relative positioning in the wine space.

> Example: Take sauvignon black and chardonnay together. Days apart they might feel like "white wine," but together they truly show how different they are.

Once a few wines have been drank simultaneously, the relative sense of where we are will transform into a better absolute sense of where we are and it will easier to taste any wine quickly.

## Temperature

Drink red colder than you think and white warmer than you think. The easiest way of managing this is to place the red wine in the refrigerator for half an hour before drinking it and take the white out before half an hour before serving it.

|Temperature|Red|White|
|-|-|-|
|Less than 18|Serve as is|Take from the fride half an hour before serving|
|Between 18 and 21|Put it in the fridge for 30 minutes to one hour|Take it from the fridge a few minutes before serving
|More than 21|Put it in the fridge several hours before serving|Take it from the fridge shortly before serving|

## Pairing

Food can change the experience of the wine strongly. A subtle white wine will taste like water when eating strong food. There is no universal model for what is good and what is bad, so the better way of understanding is to consider how things affect each other based on our own taste.

- Riesling is more acidic than most grapes. Use it when wanting to get a palate cleanser between spoons of food.
- Oaky chardonnay, Chillean and Spanish wines labled as crianza/reserva/gran reserva taste like vanilla with lower acidity. Use them when trying to get round autimmy notes.

## Year and aging

The specific year a wine was made is not very relevant thanks to the agricultural advances. However, the year is relevant in terms of how long ago the wine was made given that some compounds appear only with age and other dissapear quickly. Therefore, it is less relevant to think whether the vintage was 2025 or 2005 specifically and more in terms of whether it was made recently or long ago.

|How long ago|Clues|
|-|-|
|Less than two years|All the fruty notes are still there|
|Between two and five years|A good proportion of the fruit has dissapeared. Some of the tannins have softened|
|Between five and 10|The weird years|
|More than 10|Most fruity notes have dissapeared. Less tannins. More smells like leather.|
